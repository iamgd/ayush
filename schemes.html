  • • National AYUSH Mission (NAM):
    This mission aims to promote and develop AYUSH systems of medicine, including Ayurveda, Yoga, Naturopathy, Unani, Siddha, and Homeopathy.
    It provides support for infrastructure, research, and education in these fields.

  • • AYUSH Health and Wellness Centers (HWCs):
    The government has initiated the establishment of AYUSH HWCs to provide traditional healthcare services at the community level, focusing on preventive and promotive healthcare.

  • • National Medicinal Plants Board (NMPB):
    This board promotes the cultivation and conservation of medicinal plants used in AYUSH systems of medicine and provides financial assistance to farmers and organizations involved in this sector.

  • • Swasthya Rakshan Programme:
    This program focuses on preventive healthcare through the promotion of AYUSH practices and interventions for various diseases.

  • • Research and Development in AYUSH:
    Multiple schemes and initiatives fund research in Ayurveda, Yoga, Unani, Siddha, and Homeopathy to encourage scientific validation and modernization of traditional systems of medicine.

  • • Ayurveda, Yoga, and Naturopathy, Unani, Siddha, and Homeopathy (AYUSH) Education:
    Various programs support the education and training of healthcare professionals in AYUSH systems.

  • • National Institute of Open Schooling (NIOS):
    NIOS offers AYUSH-related courses and certification programs, making AYUSH education more accessible.

  • • Rashtriya Kishor Swasthya Karyakram (RKSK):
    This program focuses on adolescent health and wellbeing, incorporating AYUSH practices to promote holistic health among teenagers.

  • • AYUSH Hospitals and Clinics:
    Initiatives to establish specialized hospitals and clinics dedicated to AYUSH systems, offering comprehensive healthcare services.

  • • Integrative Medicine Programs:
    Efforts to integrate AYUSH practices with modern medicine, promoting a synergistic approach for better patient care and treatment outcomes.

  • • AYUSH Telemedicine Services:
    Implementing telemedicine platforms to provide remote consultations and healthcare services based on AYUSH principles, ensuring accessibility to rural areas.

  • • AYUSH Awareness Campaigns:
    Campaigns and public outreach programs aimed at raising awareness about AYUSH systems, their benefits, and their role in promoting health and wellness.
